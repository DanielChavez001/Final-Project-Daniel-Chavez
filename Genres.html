Genres

Action

This Genre of books constantly have you on the edge of your seat with excitement, as your fave main character repeatedly finds themselves in high stakes situations.

Adventure

The protagonist has an ultimate goal to achieve and is always put in risky, often dangerous situations.

Grapic Novel

The stories in comic books and graphic novels are presented to the reader through engaging, sequential narrative art (illustrations and typography) that's either presented in a specific design or the traditional panel layout you find in comics.

Mystery

The plot always revolves around a crime of sorts that must be solved—or foiled—by the protagonists.

Fantasy

While usually set in a fictional imagined world including prominent elements of magic, mythology, or the supernatural.

Historical Fiction

These books are based in a time period set in the past decades, often against the backdrop of significant (real) historical events.

Horror

Meant to cause discomfort and fear for both the character and readers, horror writers often make use of supernatural and paranormal elements in morbid stories that are sometimes a little too realistic.

Romance

The genre that makes your heart all warm and fuzzy focuses on the love story of the main protagonists. This world of fiction is extremely wide-reaching in and of itself, as it has a variety of sub-genres including: contemporary romance, historical, paranormal, and the steamier erotica.

Science Fiction (Sci-Fi)

Though they're often thought of in the same vein as fantasy, what distinguishes science fiction stories is that they lean heavily on themes of technology and future science. You'll find apocalyptic and dystopian novels in the sci-fi genre as well.
